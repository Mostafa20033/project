INTRODUCTION

France is a constitutional republic with a centralized and parliamentary system with a presidential tendency, with a population of about 66 million people, and it is located in Western Europe, and it has several regions and territories scattered all over the world. Its capital is Paris, and its language is the French, and its official currency is the Euro , brothers), its flag is made of three vertical colors in order blue, white and red, its national anthem is La Marseille. 1)Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is an iron tower with a height of 324 meters, located in Paris, in the far north-west of the Champs-de-Mars park, near the Seine River. Two years, two months and 5 days (1887-1889), and it was opened to the masses on May 15, 1889. It was called the 300-meter tower at the opening, and this facility became the symbol of the French capital and the first tourist symbol in it, and it represents the ninth most visited French site in 2006, and it is also the first landmark of Where the number of visitors as the number of visitors is 7 million visitors annually. With a height of 313.2 metres, the Eiffel Tower was for 41 years the tallest landmark in the world. Its height has been increased several times by installing several antennas, reaching a height of 327 meters since March 8, 2011.It was used in the past in many scientific experiments, and is used today in broadcasting radio and television programs.

2)DISNEYLAND

Disneyland Resort Paris or Euro Disney is the largest amusement park complex in Europe. Fit for both young and old. It is located in Paris, France, just 32 kilometers from the center of Paris in an area called "Marne-la-Vallée". The Disneyland Paris product includes two theme parks in addition to seven Disney-owned hotels. Opened on April 12, 1992, it was the second Disney park to open outside the United States after Tokyo Disneyland. Hundreds of thousands of tourists visit it annually, where they find various amusement parks for adults and children: swings, snake and water slides, and mini-car races for adults and children in which they spend a day or two where parents and their children enjoy the beautiful atmosphere, imaginary buildings from legends, architectural art distribution, green gardens and flowers in a joyful atmosphere and pleasure.

3)Louvre Museum

The Louvre Museum is the largest art museum in the world and the largest historical monument in the city of Paris, located on the right bank of the Seine River. The Louvre contains about 38,000 pieces dating from prehistoric times to the twenty-first century. The Louvre was the most visited art museum in the world in 2018, receiving 10.2 million visitors. This museum is located inside the Louvre Palace, which was built on Castle foundation in the late twelfth to thirteenth centuries

4)Arc de Triomphe

One of the most famous tourist places in Paris, and work began in the nineteenth century, when Napoleon Bonaparte wanted a symbol to immortalize the victories of the imperial armies, but the actual achievement took place in 1836, with a height of 49.50 meters, and its interior walls bear 660 names of the names of military leaders and 96 of the names of His victories The French also built after World War I a tomb for the Unknown Soldier with a permanent torch at the bottom of the arch in memory of him. The arch is one of the most important landmarks in Paris.

5)d'Orsay Museum

The Musée d'Orsay is not only one of the most important landmarks of Paris and one of the most famous landmarks of France, but one of the largest and most famous European museums that has given special priority to the field of plastic arts; Where the museum displays the best works and drawings by international artists such as Monet, Van Gogh, Cézanne and others, through more than 2000 paintings and 600 sculptural pieces of the greatest European artists during the period from 1848 to 1914.

6)Palais Garnier or Paris Opera

It is an amazing building of very beauty that was founded by “Louis XIV” in 1669 on the orders of Napoleon III for the reconstruction of Paris. Opera opened in 1875 contains 2,200 seating seats and a bronze crystal chandelier weighing about 7 tons. It is one of the most important tourist places in Paris.
